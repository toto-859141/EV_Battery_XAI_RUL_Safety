# Contribution Statement

## Contribution Statement

This study is expected to make five interrelated contributions to research on electric vehicle battery analytics, with particular emphasis on the joint consideration of remaining useful life prediction, safety risk prediction, and explainable artificial intelligence.

### Methodological Contribution

The study is expected to contribute a structured methodological framework that aligns data design, target-label definition, feature engineering, model development, explainability, and evaluation within a single analytical pipeline. Its methodological value lies in treating `target_rul` and `target_safety_risk` as distinct dependent variables and in evaluating candidate models not only by predictive performance, but also by interpretability, robustness, and safety relevance.

### Application Contribution

The study is expected to contribute an application-oriented predictive framework for electric vehicle battery monitoring that uses routinely observable electrical, thermal, degradation-related, and operational variables to support prognostic and safety-oriented analysis. In applied terms, the work is intended to show how battery monitoring data can be organized into a reproducible workflow for generating actionable predictions rather than isolated analytical outputs.

### Explainability Contribution

The study is expected to contribute a more rigorous use of explainable artificial intelligence in battery research by applying explanation methods to compare the predictor structure of two different outcomes: remaining useful life and safety risk. This contribution is important because it moves beyond treating explainability as a post hoc descriptive step and instead uses it as an analytic basis for identifying shared predictors, task-specific predictors, and technically plausible model behavior.

### Safety Contribution

The study is expected to contribute a safety-aware interpretation framework for battery prediction by explicitly addressing the meaning of elevated safety-risk outputs, the relationship between safety risk and RUL, and the consequences of safety-relevant prediction errors. In particular, the work is expected to strengthen methodological attention to false negatives, conservative warning logic, and the limits of proxy-based safety labels in battery datasets.

### Academic Contribution

The study is expected to contribute to the academic literature by clarifying the conceptual distinction among battery health, remaining useful life, and safety risk, which are often discussed in overlapping terms despite representing different analytical constructs. It is also expected to provide a thesis-ready basis for future empirical work by offering a coherent research design, explicit assumptions, and a replicable documentation structure that can support later journal publication or further battery prognostics research.
