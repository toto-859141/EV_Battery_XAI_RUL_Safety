# Inclusion and Exclusion Criteria

## Purpose

This document defines the screening criteria for the literature review. The goal is to ensure that the final review is academically rigorous, thematically relevant, and based on traceable scholarly sources.

## Inclusion Criteria

A paper should be included in the formal review if it meets all of the following criteria.

### 1. Scholarly Source with DOI

- The paper is an existing scholarly publication.
- A DOI is available and can be verified.

### 2. Topical Relevance

The paper addresses at least one of the following themes:

- EV battery remaining useful life prediction;
- battery safety risk prediction;
- battery health monitoring;
- explainable AI for prognostics; or
- interpretable machine learning for safety-critical systems.

### 3. Substantive Technical Content

The paper contains at least one of the following:

- a predictive model;
- a diagnostic or monitoring framework;
- an explainability or interpretability method;
- a dataset-based empirical evaluation; or
- a methodological discussion directly relevant to the project.

### 4. Relevance to the Research Framing

The paper contributes to at least one of the following project needs:

- definition of `target_rul`;
- definition of safety-related outcomes;
- battery health indicators or monitoring variables;
- predictive modeling methods;
- interpretability or explainability methods;
- safety-critical model evaluation or justification.

## Exclusion Criteria

A paper should be excluded from the formal review if any of the following conditions apply.

### 1. No Verifiable DOI

- The paper has no DOI.
- The DOI cannot be verified.

### 2. Irrelevant Scope

- The paper is not related to batteries, prognostics, safety prediction, health monitoring, explainability, or safety-critical interpretability.
- The paper concerns topics outside the methodological scope of the project without providing transferable insight.

### 3. Insufficient Technical Detail

- The paper is purely editorial, promotional, or descriptive without substantive analytical content.
- The paper does not describe the dataset, variables, methods, or findings sufficiently for synthesis.

### 4. Duplicates or Redundant Versions

- The record is a duplicate of an already included study.
- A conference and journal version of essentially the same study are both retrieved, and one version is clearly more complete.

### 5. Weak Relevance to the Review Questions

- The paper mentions explainability, safety, or prognostics only superficially.
- The paper cannot reasonably inform the research gap, objectives, variables, methods, or conceptual framing of the project.

## Priority Ranking for Included Studies

If the number of potentially relevant studies is large, prioritize papers that satisfy more of the following conditions:

- directly address electric vehicle or lithium-ion batteries;
- include empirical modeling of RUL, safety, or health;
- define targets clearly;
- report datasets and predictor variables explicitly;
- use interpretable or explainable machine learning methods;
- discuss limitations relevant to safety-critical deployment.

## Borderline Cases

### Adjacent-Domain XAI Studies

Studies from adjacent safety-critical domains may be included only if:

- they have a DOI;
- they contribute clearly to the methodological theme of interpretable machine learning for safety-critical systems; and
- their relevance to battery analytics is stated explicitly in the review notes.

### Non-EV Battery Studies

Lithium-ion battery studies not explicitly focused on electric vehicles may be included when they are strongly relevant to:

- degradation prediction;
- thermal safety prediction;
- battery health estimation; or
- explainability methods applicable to EV battery systems.

The rationale for inclusion should be recorded in the matrix notes or thematic summary.

## Screening Decision Labels

For consistency, each screened paper should be assigned one of the following labels:

- `include`
- `exclude`
- `maybe`

Papers labeled `maybe` should be revisited after abstract screening or full-text review.
