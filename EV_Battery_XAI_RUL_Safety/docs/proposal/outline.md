# Proposal Outline

## 1. Title

Working title:
Explainable Artificial Intelligence for Safety Risk and Remaining Useful Life Prediction of Electric Vehicle Batteries

## 2. Background and Motivation

- Why EV battery health and safety matter
- Why RUL and safety risk should be studied together
- Why explainability is important for trust and decision support

## 3. Problem Statement

- What technical gap does this project address?
- What practical gap exists in current EV battery prognostics and safety assessment?

## 4. Research Objectives

- Develop a pipeline for EV battery data preparation and feature engineering
- Build predictive models for RUL and safety risk
- Apply XAI methods to interpret predictions and highlight influential factors

## 5. Research Questions

- Which battery features are most informative for RUL prediction?
- Which indicators are most associated with safety risk?
- How do explainability methods help validate or challenge model behavior?

## 6. Scope

In scope:

- Public or experimental battery datasets
- Supervised learning for RUL and safety-related predictions
- Post hoc explainability and model interpretation

Out of scope:

- Full battery physics simulation
- Real-time embedded deployment in the initial phase

## 7. Methodology

- Literature review
- Dataset selection and preprocessing
- Feature engineering
- Model development
- Evaluation
- Explainability analysis

## 8. Expected Outputs

- Baseline and improved predictive models
- Explainability results and visualizations
- Thesis proposal and manuscript materials

## 9. Timeline

- Month 1: literature review and dataset selection
- Month 2: preprocessing and exploratory analysis
- Month 3: baseline modeling
- Month 4: explainability analysis and writing

## 10. Risks and Mitigations

- Limited dataset quality or label availability
- Difficulty aligning RUL and safety targets
- Explainability results that are unstable across models
