## Brief Synthesis Note

Across these papers, five patterns are especially relevant to the present study:

1. RUL prediction studies are increasingly hybrid and sequence-oriented, but most remain weak on explainability and direct safety interpretation.
2. Safety and fault-diagnosis studies are stronger on early warning and operational relevance, but they often stop at anomaly detection rather than linking faults to long-horizon life prediction.
3. Battery prognostics reviews consistently emphasize the importance of data acquisition, feature engineering, uncertainty, and deployment constraints, which supports a methodology-centered thesis design.
4. XAI studies in batteries are growing, but many focus on SOH rather than on jointly interpreting RUL and safety-oriented outcomes.
5. Multi-task and hybrid studies suggest promising integration pathways, yet the evidence base for jointly modeling life prediction and safety risk in an explainable way remains limited.
