# Search Keywords and Keyword Combinations

## Purpose

This document provides a keyword framework for identifying DOI-indexed scholarly literature relevant to the project topic:

**Explainable Artificial Intelligence for Safety Risk and Remaining Useful Life Prediction of Electric Vehicle Batteries**

Searches should be conducted in scholarly databases that index DOI-bearing literature, such as Scopus, Web of Science, IEEE Xplore, ScienceDirect, SpringerLink, Wiley Online Library, or Google Scholar with DOI verification.

## Search Principles

The search strategy should combine four concept groups:

1. battery domain terms;
2. task terms related to RUL, safety, or health;
3. method terms related to machine learning or explainable AI; and
4. filtering terms for electric vehicles or lithium-ion systems where needed.

## Search Refinement Rules

### Include Broader Terms When Necessary

If the initial search returns too few battery-specific papers on explainability, broaden the search to:

- prognostics in rotating machinery or industrial systems;
- interpretable machine learning in fault diagnosis; and
- explainable AI in safety-critical engineering systems.

These studies may be included only when they contribute methodologically to the XAI or safety-critical interpretability themes and still have a DOI.

### Narrow Terms When Results Are Too Broad

If the search returns too many unrelated papers, combine:

- battery-specific terms with EV-specific terms;
- interpretability terms with prognostics terms; and
- safety terms with thermal or fault-related terms.

## Screening Reminder

A paper should not be included in the formal literature matrix unless:

- it is an existing scholarly publication;
- it is relevant to at least one review theme; and
- its DOI can be verified.
